This simulation demonstrates "diffusion limited aggregation" (DLA). It begins with a single seed particle, and one-by-one additional particles stick (or "aggregate") to form a cluster. The cluster formed by this process is an example of a "fractal". As the cluster grows in size, the number of particles in the cluster is not proportional to the radius of the cluster--as would be the case for a one-dimensional structure; and the number of particles is also not proportional to the radius squared--as would be the case for a two-dimensional structure. Instead, the number of particles is proportional to the radius raised to a non-integer power, referred to as the "fractal dimension".
