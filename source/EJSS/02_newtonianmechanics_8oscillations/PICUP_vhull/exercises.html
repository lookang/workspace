Exercise 1: A straight-sided boat

Imagine a boat with vertical sides, floating. There will be two forces on this boat: its weight mgmg, and the buoyant force −ρgV−ρgV. (I’ve defined down to be the positive direction, here, so the buoyant force is negative since it’s upwards.) The volume VV in the buoyant force component is the volume of water displaced by the boat: in other words, the volume of boat that is below water level. In equilibrium, the bottom of the boat will be some distance xoxo below the water, so V=AxoV=Axo, where AA is the area of the boat at the waterline.

If we then push the boat down some additional distance xx and let go, the boat will bob up and down.

  1. Show that the boat’s vertical motion is SHO. It will probably be helpful to follow the same pattern as for the introduction example: the key is to get the equation in the form of the equation for SHO.
  2. What is the period of the boat’s motion?
