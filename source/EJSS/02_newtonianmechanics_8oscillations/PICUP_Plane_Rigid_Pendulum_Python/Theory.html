If we neglect forces that produce any damping or driving torques, then the bar’s weight is the only force exerted on the bar that produces a torque about point P. We shall use a sign convention such that any torque that tries to produce an angular acceleration in the counterclockwise direction contributes a positive amount to the total torque acting on the bar, and any torque that tries to accelerate the bar in the clockwise direction is a negative contribution.
