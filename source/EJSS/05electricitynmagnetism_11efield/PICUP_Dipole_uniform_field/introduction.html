Electric Dipole in an Uniform Field

Developed by David Urminsky

Easy JavaScript Simulation by Wei Chiong Tan and Loo Kang Wee

This set of exercises will guide students through calculating the torque on an electric dipole in a uniform electric field. It will then have the students use the small angle approximation to show that the dipole will undergo simple harmonic motion for a small perturbation from the stable equilibrium. Finally, the students will explore the concept of how small is small for the small angle approximation to provide the correct dynamics.

Subject Area Electricity & Magnetism
Level First Year
Available Implementation IPython/Jupyter Notebook
Easy JavaScript Simulation
Learning Objectives

Upon completing this exercise set, students will be able to:

  • analytically calculate the net force and torque on an electric dipole in a uniform electric field. (Exercise 1)
  • determine stable and unstable equilibrium points for the dipole. (Exercise 1)
  • understand the link between the torque on the dipole with the dipole moment (Exercise 1)
  • demonstrate analytically that for a small angle, θθ, displacement from the stable equilibrium point the dipole will undergo simple harmonic motion (Exercise 2)
  • determine the angular frequency of the motion for small angle displacement. (Exercise 2)
  • calculate a numerical solution to the motion to compare with the solution with the small angle approximation(Exercise 2)
  • explore how increasing θθ breaks down the small angle approximation (Exercise 3)
