This set of exercises is appropriate for an introductory physics course for E&M. It may also be useful near the beginning of an introductory computational physics course as well.

Students should learn about the torque on a dipole and derive the expressions τ=p×Eτ=p×E and τ=pEsin(θ)τ=pEsin⁡(θ).

The students will also get to explore the small angle approximation to get simple harmonic motion.

Some code is provided which the students can modify (change parameter values) to determine how good the small angle approximation is over time.

There are two versions of the jupyter notebook for this exercise set. One uses odeint to numerically solve the ode. The other notebook explicitly uses the Euler-Cromer method to numerically solve the ode. Which one you use may depend on what you want your students to get out of the activity.
