7.1.4 Example (N84/P2/Q7) Binary Stars

Two stars of equal mass M move with constant speed v in a circular orbit of radius R about their common centre of mass O. What is the net force on each star?

A   G M 2 R 2          B     G M 2 4 R 2           C    zero          D   2 M v 2 R 2           E    M v 2 2 R

7.1.4.1 Activity:

select from the down-drop menu "M1=M2=1,circular_orbit"

click play and observe the motion, noting down the magnitude of the forces (green arrows) on each mass.

let assume in the simulation that  M 1 = 1 s . m . = 1.988 x 1 0 30 k g    and M 2 = 1  s . m . = 1.988 x 1 0 30 k g

given that astronomical unit is 1 a.u. = 1.496x1011 m

the distance between the masses is   R 1 + R 2 = 2 a .u. = ( 2 ) ( 1.496 x 1 0 11 ) m

the force is therefore, F 1 = G M 1 M 2 ( R 1 + R 2 ) 2 = ( 6.67 x 1 0 -11 ) ( 1.988 x 1 0 30 ) ( 1.988 x 1 0 30 ) ( ( 2 ) ( 1.496 x 1 0 11 ) ) 2 = 2.94 x 1 0 27 N


therefore net force on each M1 star is F 1 = G M 2 4 R 2

similarly, the net force on M2 star is F 2 = G M 2 4 R 2 making the answer B

in  the simulation terms, notice the force on M1,  F1 = (2.98x1026)9.81 N and F2 = (2.98x1026)9.81 N.
