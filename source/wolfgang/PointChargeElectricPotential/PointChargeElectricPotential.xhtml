Point Charge Electric Potential

The Point Charge Electric Potential model shows the potential from multiple point charges. Use the add charge buttons to create additional point charges and drag them to create various configurations. The potential at a point is equal to the work done by an external agent in carrying a unit of positive charge from infinity to that point without any acceleration. Regions where the potential is positive are shown in red and regions where the potential is negative are shown in blue. The exact value of the potential U is shown in a yellow message box when click-dragging within the view. You can observe the point-particle singularity if you drag close to a point charge. A good way to visualize the potential is to color code positive potential.

The surface on which the electric potential has an equal value everywhere is called an equipotential surface. It is a shown as a white line (contour) in our two-dimensional model. Equipotential lines (surfaces) with different potential values cannot cross although the simulation may show crossing lines if the number of grid points n is too small.

Teaching Objectives

  • Learning Goal

    To introduce electric potential from one or more point charges.

  • Essential Knowledge

    Although only the potential difference between two points can be measured, choosing a reference point (usually at infinity) allows us define a potential throughout space. The potential at any point relative to the reference point depends only on the charges and their location.
